Can't Let the Other Girls Down

My Experience with Stereotype Threat

April 5, 2015

I sometimes feel worried that if I don't catch onto the materials we cover in Dev Bootcamp quickly enough, then I'll reinforce the stereotype that girls can't code. For better or worse, I also feel that if I ever gave up on learning to code, I'd let my entire gender down. That kind of thinking is irrational, so I dismiss those thoughts as soon as they surface. But they still try to bubble up every now and then. This week, all Dev Bootcamp students learned the word for this phenomenon: stereotype threat.

Stereotype threat is "the threat of being viewed through the lens of a negative stereotype, or the fear of doing something that would inadvertently confirm that stereotype." - Thin Ice: Stereotype Threat and Black College Students
